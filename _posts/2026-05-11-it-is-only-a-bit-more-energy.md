title: It's only a bit more energy
---

<!--more-->

Ron Jeffries in ["Addiction Is Not a Sin"](https://ronjeffries.com/articles/-v026/v/y/):

> LLM-“AI” is a destroyer of the environment, a violator of copyright law, a machine that takes the jobs of people who need them, a tool of the oligarchy, a pump moving money from all of us toward the rich. That’s enough to keep me as far from the things as I can get.

[Cryptocurrencies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryptocurrency), an application of a "shiny new"[^1] technology like [blockchain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockchain), have received a lot of criticism for their [excessive use of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_impact_of_bitcoin). This and their dangerous speculative nature made them fell into a sort of niche market and eventually lose mainstream popularity.

Training and subsequent use of [Large Language Models](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_language_model) (LLMs) have also contributed to a significant increase in electricity consumption[^2], even when we consider the [challenges for measuring its actual impact](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1364032125008329) across regions.

And yet, they don't seem to get as much bad reputation as cryptocurrencies. I've always wondered why that was the case.

Some people I've talked to say that, compared to cryptocurrencies, the increased use of energy by LLMs is justified because we get much more value out of them. It's just the price of technological progress.

Others, the more optimistic and possibly also uninformed, say that previously developed technologies already consume a lot of energy and that the increase by LLMs is not such a big problem comparatively.

A third group of people, in which you can count me, is deeply concerned about the impact of LLMs on the environment because, meanwhile, [our glaciers are melting](https://ethz.ch/en/news-and-events/eth-news/news/2025/12/the-alps-to-lose-record-number-of-glaciers-in-the-next-decade.html) and extreme weather events like rainfalls and droughts are seemingly more frequent.

From a statistical point of view, I can't really tell how many people think one way or another. In my circles at least, a clear majority holds one of the first two views. I expected this considering that many of them either work on or have close contact with the IT[^3] world.

But reading Ron Jeffries' statement helped me connect the dots. My guess is that LLMs don't get the same treatment as "destroyers of the environment" like cryptocurrencies because they're being ~~shovelled down our throats~~ promoted by oligarch [tech-bros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tech_bro).

Cryptocurrencies, in contrast, having been born mostly as a libertarian or anarchist tool to circumvent the establishment, never seemed to get enough support from them. Perhaps because they didn't want to get associated with right-wing extremists like Steve Bannon, or because they considered the technology as fringe and outside their control.

But the scenario with LLMs is different, and the propaganda machine of Silicon Valley and the likes is making sure to praise the virtues of AI and downplay any negative impact as much as possible. Obviously, only until a few winners get their money and the disruption to the labour market leaves thousands or millions of workers behind.

[^1]: In reality the ideas behind blockchain had been proposed as early as 1982.

[^2]: Not to mention the use of water to cool down data centres, plus other [environmental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_impact_of_artificial_intelligence) issues.

[^3]: Many, but not only, software engineers in particular seem to be attracted to LLMs like moths to a flame 🙄
